About the project

Spazio Progettato, Spazio Vissuto is my Master's thesis in Interior and Spatial Design at Politecnico di Milano. It investigates the relationship between design and lived experience in public space, starting from a fundamental premise: since it is impossible to design people's behaviour and intentions, there is an unavoidable gap between the space as designed and the space as experienced. The thesis argues that this gap can be reduced through conscious design practices that make space welcoming and encourage social interaction.

Research & methodology

The work is structured in two parts. The first builds a theoretical framework around the key components of public space: participation, perception, atmosphere, aesthetics, and behaviour. The second is a collection of built projects, analysed to identify how public space is designed, experienced, and interpreted in practice. The methodology combines historical-critical analysis with case study research.

The thesis concludes that the quality of public space does not depend exclusively on its formal configuration, but on a project's ability to create environmental and relational conditions that favour plural uses, permanence, recognisability, and a sense of belonging, arguing for a shift from design as form to design as condition.

Activities & skills

I conducted the entire theoretical research independently, from the selection and analysis of bibliographic sources to the construction of the conceptual framework. I also took full responsibility for the editorial design of the thesis: from the format and font selection to the layout, the balance between images and text, the choice of paper, and the cover design.
