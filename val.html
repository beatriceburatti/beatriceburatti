About the project

The studio aimed to explore the principles of shared economy and circular design through the development of a product–service system for everyday use, focusing on rethinking ownership, durability, and use in response to contemporary mobility and temporary living.

Within this framework, VAL investigates mobility as a permanent condition rather than a temporary phase. By transforming a suitcase into an adaptable storage system, the project addresses the challenge of inhabiting transitional spaces and proposes more flexible and sustainable ways of living.

Concept

Starting from the experience as students living abroad for a few months, we encountered the challenges of temporary living: limited space, economic accessibility, lack of storage, and the difficulty of feeling at home in transitional environments.

By working with durable, affordable, and recyclable materials, we designed VAL as a modular system redefining the suitcase from a passive container into an active element of living. Its transformable configuration allows the object to open, unfold, and recompose itself, offering storage, organization, and personal expression within temporary spaces. VAL encourages customization and reuse: through its circular product–service model, it extends beyond single ownership through shared circulation.
