About the project

The studio aimed to explore the concept of courage through the design of an environmental object. We interpreted courage as the capacity to welcome others into our space, as well as step into theirs.

This translates into the somewhat metaphorical yet highly pragmatic concept of trespassing cultural, social, and personal boundaries that impact our daily lives and define our limits. The object challenges traditional perceptions, inviting users to engage thoughtfully and creatively with the complexities of borders and drawing strength from that.

The installation was exhibited to the public during Milano Design Week 2025 at Design Variations 2025 by MoscaPartners.

Concept

Using soft and welcoming materials, we wanted to create a space for connection and reflection. After identifying nets as our conceptual framework, we had to design a structure that could complement the idea of the passage with the transparency of surfaces.

It was about imagining a space to perceive an obstacle to overcome but also to feel the will and desire to trespass it. Among many alternatives, we selected the corridor shape as we felt it could allow the visitor to both be still and move along with the patterns of the net, looking outside.
